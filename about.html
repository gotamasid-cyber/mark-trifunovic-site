About

"Life moves pretty fast. If you don't stop and look around once in a while, you could miss it."

I'm a filmmaker originally from Leeds in northern England. Everybody there sounds like they're from Winterfell. I now live and work in Toronto, Canada. I flew here in 2014 on a one way ticket. It was one of the best decisions I ever made.

I lead something of a double life in film. When I'm not dressing sets or doing props for TV shows and commercials I'm writing and directing films of my own. I'm not prejudiced about format: if I like an idea, I'll take an opportunity.

Over the last few years I've been working with the Deaf community in Toronto, collaborating with artists and filmmakers in American Sign Language. In 2016, I wrote and directed Deafening Darkness, Toronto's first all Deaf cast horror movie. In 2020, I made Old Mother Tongue, a short film about the 1880 Milan Congress on the Education of the Deaf.

I'm keen to expand my network of collaborators — writers, producers, musicians, and creative agencies. Let's put our minds to this and see what we can create together.
